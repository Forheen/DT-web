Why we build

Rural women carry their communities. The systems around them cannot see them.

A rural woman repays her SHG loan on time, every month, for six years. She grows and sells produce. She runs a side income. She mentors three others. And yet — to a bank, a government scheme, or a supply chain — she does not exist. Her economic life is real and invisible at once.

This invisibility is not a technology problem. It is a memory and infrastructure problem. The world has no system that remembers her work, recognises her capability, or helps her take the next best step toward a better life. DT and MIAF are built to change that — together.

The moral case

Her work deserves to be seen

When her economic activity leaves no record, she is denied credit she earned, schemes she qualifies for, and a say in her own future. Making her visible — on her own terms, with her consent — is simply right.

The business case

The largest unserved market in India

Tens of millions of women generate real economic value that no system can currently measure or reach. Turn that invisible activity into trusted knowledge, and you unlock a market and a movement at once.

The ecosystem

Two layers. One purpose. Enabled by DF.

MIAF builds the R&D engines — Smriti and Utkarsh. Smriti is not just a memory system: it is a full R&D platform covering voice modelling, acoustic research, Indic NLP, knowledge graph systems, and data pipelines. Utkarsh is the optimized decision engine built on Smriti's data. DT builds the interfaces: GOI is the flagship institutional analytics product; Shruti is DT's public-facing character — the voice and chatbot persona rural women know and trust, built on Smriti and Utkarsh underneath. Spandnaad and other DT platforms draw on MIAF's engines for intelligence. DF forms MIAF, anchors it institutionally, and holds 49%.

DF's 49% stake in MIAF is its institutional stake as the founding body — not a passive holding. DT — Drishtee Technology

The commercial layer — built for rural women's prosperity.

GOI — Gram Operating Interface — is DT's flagship product. Think of it as Google Analytics for rural operations, but conversational. Anyone inside the Drishtee ecosystem can ask GOI: which villages need a new hub? Why are sales declining? What is in demand where? GOI makes the full intelligence of the MIAF engines accessible in plain language, to anyone who needs it.

Alongside GOI, DT builds Shruti — not an engine, but a character. Shruti is the public-facing voice and chatbot persona that rural women interact with directly. She has a name, a personality, and speaks in their language. She is how the intelligence of Smriti and Utkarsh reaches the last mile — not as a dashboard or a query tool, but as a trusted voice a woman can talk to. Shruti is DT's most important promotional and engagement asset.

DT's commercial sustainability and rural women's economic prosperity are the same goal expressed differently. Every Dhavak route creates a livelihood. Every COS order connects a rural woman to a supply chain she couldn't reach alone. Every HUB creates employment. Every Karmasetu user is a woman whose productivity is recognised. A self-sustaining commercial layer is the most durable thing DT can give rural women — more durable than any grant cycle.

The motive

DT builds commercial products because the alternative — aid-dependent infrastructure that disappears when the funding does — has failed rural communities for decades. Every ₹1 of DT's recurring revenue is a ₹1 that does not depend on a donor's continued generosity. GOI is how that recurring revenue scales: one product, many villages, compounding intelligence.

MIAF — Memory Intelligence · R&D Engine · Formed by DF · Starts Today

The R&D engine beneath everything. Built once. Powering many.

MIAF is not a product company. It is an R&D engine — the intelligence infrastructure that DT's interfaces sit on top of, and that any other research engine or partner organization can license. Smriti researches. Utkarsh decides. Together they make every interface that consumes them smarter over time.

DT's GOI and Shruti (the public character) are MIAF's first consumers. Spandnaad — DT's HR platform — can draw on MIAF's engines for workforce intelligence and pattern recognition. As MIAF's memory grows richer with real rural data, the value it provides to every interface that plugs in compounds. This is what makes MIAF a research and commercial asset simultaneously.

MIAF is incorporated today by DF. Forheen is Founder and CEO. She holds 1% founding equity from day one. Over six months she builds Smriti and Utkarsh alongside her existing DT responsibilities — with a one-time ₹1L expense from DF's founding grant. She delivers the MVP by July 2026, moves to MIAF full-time, gains 10% equity, and draws ₹2L/month as founder salary from MIAF. Over the six months that follow, milestone-based vesting brings her stake to 51% — her founder stake on the journey to a ₹100 crore MIAF.

The positioning

OpenAI made intelligence accessible. Sarvam is making language accessible. MIAF will make memory persistent. Starting with rural Bharat — the hardest proving ground — then for every organization that needs to remember and decide well.

MIAF — what gets built

Two engines. What they are, what they do, how they work.

Smriti and Utkarsh are not generic AI wrappers. Each is a purpose-built system with a defined architecture, a specific data model, and a clear job. Below is what gets designed and built during the six-month R&D period — and what it becomes once deployed.

R&D engine
Smriti
It researches. It models. It builds the intelligence substrate.

Smriti is MIAF's core R&D engine — not just a memory store, but a research platform. It covers voice modelling, acoustic feature extraction, Indic language NLP, knowledge graph systems, data pipelines, and pattern recognition. Everything Utkarsh reasons over, and everything DT's interfaces consume, originates in Smriti's research infrastructure.

On dilution and future funding

Forheen's 51% is her founder's right — not subject to dilution until the ₹100 crore milestone. If significant seeding, institutional grants, or venture investment arrives before that milestone, the structure of any dilution is negotiated at that point — with a company that has already proved its value, not before. DF's 49% follows the same principle. Both parties build toward the milestone first; the capital conversation happens from a position of strength.
