Why we begin here

Bharat's women hold up its villages. Its systems cannot see them.

The loan repaid on time. The crop sold at the mandi. The skill learned, the household carried, the group led. A rural woman's economic life is real — and almost entirely invisible to the institutions that could fund, serve, or uplift her. That invisibility is both a wrong to correct and an opportunity no one has captured.

The moral case

A life that deserves to be seen

When her work leaves no record, she is denied credit she has earned, schemes she qualifies for, and a say in her own future. Making her visible, on her own terms and with her consent, is simply right.

The business case

The largest market no one can see

Tens of millions of women generate real economic value that no system can currently measure or reach. Turn that invisible activity into trusted, structured knowledge, and you unlock a market and a movement at once.

What MIAF does

To serve them, we built a harder thing: an R&D engine for rural intelligence.

Today's AI forgets everything the moment a conversation ends. The industry's answer is larger context windows. Our answer is structured, persistent, evidence-backed memory — built through genuine R&D in voice, language, knowledge graphs, and rural data systems. So MIAF is a memory and decision intelligence R&D company — not a language-model company, not a chatbot company. We build two engines: Smriti to research and remember, Utkarsh to decide.

01

R&D, not wrappers

Real intelligence infrastructure requires real research — in voice, language, knowledge systems, and the rural contexts others ignore.

02

Evidence, not just answers

Every memory carries where it came from — source, consent, and time. Knowledge you can trace is knowledge you can trust.

03

Decisions, not dashboards

Memory only matters if it helps someone act. Our engines turn what's remembered into the next best step forward.

The products

Two engines. Plain terms.

Smriti is the R&D engine — it researches, processes, and builds the knowledge layer. Utkarsh is the decision engine — optimized to reason over Smriti's data and recommend the next best step. Together they power everything DT builds, including Shruti — the public voice character that rural women talk to.

Smriti R&D engine
It researches. It builds.

Smriti is the intelligence substrate — a research platform covering voice modelling, Indic language NLP, knowledge graph systems, and data pipelines built for rural reality.

Every fact Smriti holds knows its source, its time, and who consented to it. Everything Utkarsh reasons over — and everything Shruti says to a woman in her village — originates here.

  • Voice & acoustic R&D
  • Indic language NLP
